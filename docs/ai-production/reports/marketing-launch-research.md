# Marketing Launch Research - Red Ball

## Source

Agent: Marketing Researcher  
Task: `TASK-0007`  
Date: 2026-05-11  
Store research checked: 2026-05-11

## Marketing Position

Red Ball should not launch as "a fun red ball adventure with obstacles." That is the exact shape of the crowded competitor set.

The strongest angle is:

**A compact physics platformer about finding the cleanest rolling line.**

Best working title direction:

- Preferred: `Red Ball: Trickline`
- Safer alternative if brand/IP risk is a concern: `Trickline Ball`

Recommended store promise:

**Roll, jump, and bounce through bite-size physics levels built for clean routes, risky coin lines, and quick mobile sessions.**

Important limitation: the current build can support "coins, hazards, bounce pads, moving platforms, patrol enemies, level select, saved progress, and hearts." It should not claim bosses, hundreds of levels, daily challenges, skins, multiplayer, leaderboards, or mastery badges unless those features are actually implemented.

## Target Audience

### Primary Audience

**Kids and family casual players**

- Likely age range: roughly 6-12, plus parents who install simple action games.
- Motivation: cute readable character, simple controls, short levels, low cognitive load.
- Store need: screenshots must prove it is understandable in 2 seconds.
- Risk: parents and kids have many red/bounce-ball choices, so generic art and generic copy will vanish.

**Casual physics-platformer players**

- Players searching for red ball, bounce ball, roller ball, ball jump, or rolling platformer games.
- Motivation: roll-jump timing, coins, hazards, simple skill improvement.
- Store need: show actual physics moments, not a menu or generic mascot pose.
- Risk: this audience is trained by clones that promise 100+ levels and bosses. Red Ball should not fight on volume.

**Completionist skill players**

- Players who replay levels to collect every coin, avoid damage, or find a cleaner route.
- Motivation: visible improvement and "one more try."
- Store need: this audience needs proof of mastery goals, not just "adventure."
- Product note: this becomes the best growth audience only after Clear / All Coins / Clean Run or Trickline-style contracts are visible in the game.

### Not The Launch Core

- Hardcore precision platformer fans expecting Celeste-like depth.
- Story-first adventure players.
- Competitive leaderboard players.
- Skin-collection players, unless cosmetics become real later.

### Category Pattern

- Direct competitors mostly sell: red/bounce/roller keywords, high level counts, bosses, lasers, skins, and "save the world" copy.
- The strongest adjacent platformers sell: control feel, authored charm, completion, combos, secrets, and replay goals.
- Red Ball's opportunity is not "more levels than them." It is "a cleaner, more readable rolling-line challenge."

## Store Page Angle

### Recommended Metadata Test

Title candidates:

- `Red Ball: Trickline`
- `Trickline Ball`
- `Red Ball Roll & Jump`

Subtitle / short tagline candidates:

- `Roll the perfect line`
- `Physics jump platformer`
- `Bounce through clean routes`

Google Play short description candidate:

`Roll, jump, and bounce through bite-size physics levels with tricky routes.`

First paragraph candidate:

`Every level is a compact stunt course. Time moving platforms, bounce over hazards, stomp patrols, collect coins, and find the cleanest route to the finish. Simple touch controls and readable physics make each run easy to start and satisfying to improve.`

Feature bullets that are currently supportable:

- Hand-built 2D physics platforming.
- Coins, hazards, bounce pads, moving platforms, and patrol enemies.
- Simple mobile touch controls.
- Level select with saved progress.
- Optional challenge: collect every coin and finish cleaner.

Claims to avoid:

- "Hundreds of levels."
- "Boss battles."
- "Daily challenges."
- "Mastery badges" or "Clean Run rewards" before implementation.
- "Offline" unless explicitly verified and intended for store use.
- "Ad-free" unless the monetization build truly supports it.
- "Best red ball game" or any vague superlative.

## 10 Short Video Hooks

Each hook should be 7-12 seconds and use real gameplay. No fake UI, no fake boss footage, no cinematic scene that is not in the game.

| # | Opening text | Clip idea | Why it works |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | `Can you land this bounce?` | Bounce pad sends the ball over a hazard into a coin line. | Shows physics and a clear skill moment instantly. |
| 2 | `The safe path is slower.` | Show a simple path, then a riskier coin route with a cleaner jump. | Makes replay value visible without claiming a feature that is not there. |
| 3 | `One moving platform. Bad timing hurts.` | Wait, roll, jump, nearly miss hazard. | Easy for viewers to understand in the first second. |
| 4 | `I missed one coin.` | Finish path, cut back to the missed coin, replay the jump. | Turns coin collection into a reason to retry. |
| 5 | `Tiny patrol, perfect stomp.` | Jump on a patrol enemy and bounce forward. | Adds action variety beyond rolling. |
| 6 | `This jump looks too early.` | Start a jump before a platform arrives, land cleanly. | Good comment bait because timing feels debatable. |
| 7 | `No brakes, just line.` | Momentum sequence over platforms and bounce pad. | Supports the Trickline positioning. |
| 8 | `The level is easy until here.` | Calm opening, then one tight hazard sequence. | Honest difficulty framing. |
| 9 | `Thumb controls check.` | Show left thumb movement plus jump button, then immediate gameplay payoff. | Reduces mobile-control anxiety. |
| 10 | `Clean run or reset?` | A near-perfect run interrupted by one small mistake. | Works especially well if Clean Run badges are implemented later. |

## Screenshot Concepts

Launch screenshots should be gameplay-first. Avoid empty landscapes, menus as the first image, or huge copy blocks.

1. **Core action**
   - Image: red ball mid-jump between platforms with coins and a hazard visible.
   - Caption: `Roll. Jump. Bounce.`

2. **Bounce pad route**
   - Image: ball hitting a bounce pad toward a coin arc.
   - Caption: `Find the clean line`

3. **Moving platform timing**
   - Image: moving platform over danger, ball waiting or landing.
   - Caption: `Time every move`

4. **Coin challenge**
   - Image: coin trail taking a riskier route than the obvious path.
   - Caption: `Collect the risky coins`

5. **Enemy stomp**
   - Image: ball bouncing off a patrol enemy.
   - Caption: `Stomp patrols`

6. **Level select/progress**
   - Image: level select with unlocked and locked levels.
   - Caption: `Clear levels, unlock more`

7. **Mobile controls**
   - Image: gameplay with touch controls visible but not covering the action.
   - Caption: `Built for quick runs`

8. **Future mastery screenshot**
   - Image: completion screen with Clear / All Coins / Clean Run badges.
   - Caption: `Master every level`
   - Requirement: use only after this exists in-game.

## Soft Launch Checklist

### Store And Creative

- Decide whether the launch name is `Red Ball: Trickline` or a less clone-adjacent title like `Trickline Ball`.
- Check icon and screenshots against Red Ball 4 and major clones so the game does not look like impersonation.
- Capture 5 gameplay screenshots and 1 short vertical trailer from real device footage.
- Use a first screenshot that communicates physics action immediately.
- Prepare English store copy first; add Turkish localization only after the English positioning is stable.
- Do not use screenshots or descriptions for unimplemented badges, bosses, skins, or daily challenges.

### Build Readiness

- Verify the first 3 levels teach movement, jump, coins, hazards, and enemies without text-heavy explanation.
- Confirm touch controls are comfortable on small phones.
- Confirm level select and saved progress work after app restart.
- Confirm hearts do not block learning too early. If testers churn at the heart wall, hearts are damaging the platformer loop.
- Verify crash-free sessions and stable frame rate on at least 3 low/mid Android devices and 2 iOS devices before any wider test.

### Measurement

- Track install source, first launch, level start, level complete, level fail, coin collection, death cause, heart empty, session length, level unlock, and ad impression if ads exist.
- Review level 1 completion, level 3 reached, session length, D1 retention, D7 retention, crash-free sessions, and store listing conversion.
- Suggested early gates before scaling:
  - Crash-free sessions: 99% or better.
  - Level 1 completion: 70% or better.
  - Level 3 reached: 45% or better.
  - D1 retention: 30% or better for a promising casual prototype.
  - D7 retention: 8-12% or better before spending meaningfully.

### Test Rollout

- Closed test: 20-50 known testers in Turkey and the developer network.
- Android open test: start with Turkey plus one or two lower-cost Android-heavy markets such as Philippines, Indonesia, Brazil, or Mexico.
- iOS TestFlight: 25-100 testers before public launch.
- Do not buy paid traffic until the store page uses real gameplay footage and the first-session funnel is measured.
- If a tiny paid test is approved later, test only creative and store conversion first, not scale.

### Creative Testing

- Post the 10 hooks above as Shorts/Reels/TikTok clips.
- Measure first-second hold, 3-second hold, completion rate, comments about difficulty, and store clicks.
- Kill generic clips quickly. Keep clips where viewers argue about the line, timing, or missed coin.
- Recut winning clips with the first gameplay frame earlier; avoid logo-first intros.

### Decision Gates

- If store clicks are weak, the issue is likely the hook, icon, or first screenshot.
- If store conversion is weak but social hooks work, rewrite the store page around the best-performing mechanic.
- If D1 is weak after players install, fix onboarding, controls, early level friction, or hearts before more marketing.
- If players replay levels for coins, prioritize visible mastery badges.
- If "perfect line" clips outperform generic "adventure" clips, make Trickline the product identity.

## Marketing Challenges For PM

- "Post on social" is not a plan. The plan is to test specific mechanics in short clips and kill weak hooks fast.
- "Fun for everyone" is not positioning. The launch audience is casual/family platformer players, with mastery players as the upside if the product supports it.
- "Like Red Ball 4" is not a store angle. It is a discoverability clue and a brand-risk warning.
- Hearts should not be the retention center. In a physics platformer, failure is practice. Blocking practice can make the game feel worse exactly when the player is learning.
- A 13-level build should not sell volume. It should sell compact, handmade, replayable routes.

## Recommended Next Product Ask

Before serious launch scaling, implement or expose a visible mastery loop:

- Clear
- All Coins
- Clean Run

That single change would make the strongest marketing hook honest: **"Roll the perfect line."**
